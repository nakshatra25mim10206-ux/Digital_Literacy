## 📝 Reflection

For this task, I used **Canva** — a free, browser-based design tool that requires no prior graphic design experience. I chose it because it has a large library of infographic templates and allows you to export a clean, print-ready PNG without any watermark on the free plan.

My design focuses on three topics: what digital literacy actually means, safe internet practices, and building a professional online presence. I wanted the infographic to be something a first-year student could glance at in 30 seconds and walk away with two or three actionable takeaways — not a wall of text.

The part I found most difficult was deciding what to leave out. There is so much that falls under "digital literacy" that fitting it meaningfully onto one page without it feeling cramped was a real challenge. I ended up cutting a section on email etiquette because it would have made the layout too busy. That taught me something useful: good communication is as much about what you don't say as what you do.
