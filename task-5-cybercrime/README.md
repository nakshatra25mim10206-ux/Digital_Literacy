## Part A – Case Study

The full case study is saved as `casestudy.pdf` in this folder.

### Summary

**The Courier Scam** is a rapidly growing form of UPI fraud in India where scammers impersonate courier officials and law enforcement officers to manipulate victims. The victim is falsely accused of involvement in illegal activities and is pressured through fear, urgency, and authority tactics such as “digital arrest.”<br>The scam typically involves isolation of the victim, use of fake documents, and demand for money via UPI to a so-called government account. Once the payment is made, the scammers disappear, making recovery difficult.

**Who is targeted:** College students, senior citizens, online shoppers, and frequent UPI users.

**Consequences:** Immediate financial loss, emotional distress, and low chances of fund recovery.

> ⚠️ Digital arrest scams have become so widespread that national authorities have issued public warnings to raise awareness.

## Part B – Prevention Checklist

The full checklist is saved as `prevention-checklist.md` in this folder.

### Stay Safe Online – Quick Reference

| # | Tip |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Never share your UPI PIN or OTP** with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank |
| 2 | **Verify before you transfer** — always call back on the official number before acting on any "urgent" request |
| 3 | **No government agency collects money via UPI** — customs, CBI, and courts do not work this way |
| 4 | **Hang up on digital arrest calls** — no legitimate authority will keep you on a video call for hours |
| 5 | **Do not scan unknown QR codes** — especially from buyers on OLX or Facebook Marketplace |
| 6 | **Use strong, unique passwords** for all banking and UPI apps — enable biometric lock wherever possible |
| 7 | **Check app permissions** — no UPI app needs access to your contacts, camera, or microphone at all times |
| 8 | **Do not install screen-sharing apps** (AnyDesk, TeamViewer) on request from an unknown caller |
| 9 | 💰 **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your bank app to reduce potential loss |
| 10 | 💰 **Link only one account with a small balance** to your UPI app — keep savings in a separate account |
| 11 | **Log out of payment apps** on shared or borrowed devices immediately after use |
| 12 | **Report immediately** — every minute counts after a fraudulent transfer |

### 🆘 Reporting Channels

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| 📞 **Helpline 1930** | National Cyber Crime Financial Fraud Helpline — call within minutes of fraud for best chance of recovery |
| 🌐 **cybercrime.gov.in** | National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal — file a complaint online 24x7 |
| 🏦 **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Call immediately to flag the transaction and request an account freeze |

## 📝 Reflection

What surprised me most while researching this topic was not the technology involved — it is actually quite simple. What genuinely shocked me was how deeply these scams are engineered around human psychology. The scammers know exactly how to trigger fear, isolate the victim, and create urgency in a way that shuts down rational thinking. I read testimonies of engineers, doctors, and professors who fell for these scams — people far more experienced than me. That was humbling.

The one habit I will personally change: **I will never approve or initiate a UPI transfer when I am feeling pressured, scared, or rushed.** If someone is creating urgency around money, that urgency itself is the warning sign. I will always hang up, take a breath, and verify independently — no matter how convincing the caller sounds.
