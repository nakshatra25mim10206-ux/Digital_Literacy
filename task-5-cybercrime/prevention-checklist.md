# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 5 | Nakshatra Kundnani | 25MIM10206

> A practical guide for college students in India to protect themselves from UPI fraud and online cybercrime.

---

## 🔐 General Online Safety

- [ ] Use a **strong, unique password** for every app and account — avoid using your name, birthdate, or "123456"
- [ ] Enable **two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all banking, email, and social media accounts
- [ ] **Log out** of UPI and banking apps on shared, borrowed, or public devices immediately after use
- [ ] Regularly **check your linked bank accounts and UPI transaction history** for any unfamiliar activity
- [ ] Never install **unverified apps** from outside the official Play Store or App Store

---

## 💸 UPI & Financial Safety

- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or mPIN** with anyone — not family, not bank staff, not "government officials"
- [ ] Understand the difference: **receiving money never requires you to enter your PIN** — if someone asks you to, it is a scam
- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your bank app to cap potential losses in case of fraud
- [ ] Keep your **savings in a separate account** — link only an account with a small balance to your UPI app
- [ ] **Verify the recipient's UPI ID** carefully before confirming any transfer, especially on OLX or social media marketplaces
- [ ] **Never scan a QR code** sent by an unknown buyer or seller — QR codes can initiate payments out of your account, not into it

---

## 📞 Protecting Yourself from Scam Calls

- [ ] **No government agency — CBI, customs, police, or any court — will ever call you and ask for money via UPI.** This is always a scam.
- [ ] If someone claims you are "under digital arrest" or must stay on a video call — **hang up immediately.** This is not a real legal procedure.
- [ ] **Never follow instructions from an unknown caller** to install AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or any screen-sharing app
- [ ] If a caller creates extreme urgency ("you have 10 minutes or you will be arrested") — **stop, breathe, and call a trusted person first**
- [ ] Use **Truecaller or a similar app** to identify unknown numbers before answering

---

## 🌐 Social Media & Digital Footprint

- [ ] **Review your privacy settings** on Instagram, WhatsApp, and Facebook — limit who can see your phone number, location, and posts
- [ ] Do not post your **daily schedule, hostel address, or travel plans** publicly — this information can be used to target you
- [ ] **Think before you post** — ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?"
- [ ] Do not accept **friend or follow requests from strangers** without verifying their identity
- [ ] Use your **college email only for academic platforms** — keep it separate from personal social accounts

---

## 🖥️ Device & Account Security

- [ ] Keep your **phone's operating system and apps updated** — updates often include critical security patches
- [ ] **Review app permissions** regularly — a UPI app does not need access to your contacts, camera, or microphone at all times
- [ ] Enable **screen lock (PIN, pattern, or biometric)** on your phone at all times
- [ ] Do not connect to **unknown or public Wi-Fi networks** when using banking or UPI apps — use mobile data instead
- [ ] **Back up important data** to a secure cloud account so you do not lose it in case of device theft or ransomware

---

## 🆘 If You Have Been Scammed — Act Immediately

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| **1. Call 1930** | National Cyber Crime Financial Fraud Helpline — available 24x7. The sooner you call, the higher the chance of freezing the fraudulent account before the money moves. |
| **2. File a complaint online** | Visit **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)** and file a report. You will receive a complaint number for tracking. |
| **3. Call your bank** | Contact your bank's fraud helpline immediately and request a transaction freeze or reversal. |
| **4. Save all evidence** | Screenshot the scammer's number, UPI ID, any messages, and the transaction details before they disappear. |
| **5. Tell someone you trust** | Do not stay silent out of shame — tell a parent, friend, or college counsellor. Reporting quickly is the only way to recover money. |
