## 📝 Reflection

Working on this task introduced me to two very different but equally important categories of digital tools. HackerRank was honestly more fun than I expected — solving even a basic challenge gave me a real sense of progress and made me want to attempt harder problems. Seeing a badge appear after the Python test felt rewarding, and I can see how these profiles matter to recruiters.

Building the Google Form was a different kind of exercise. I had to think carefully about what questions would actually test awareness versus just general knowledge. Designing the quiz from the perspective of a Digital Ambassador — someone whose job is to educate peers — made me think harder about clarity and structure than I would have otherwise.

Both tools will be genuinely useful in my academic life: HackerRank for building a verifiable coding portfolio, and Google Forms for group projects, feedback collection, and event organisation within college.
