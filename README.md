### 🔐 Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide
> **Folder:** [`task-5-cybercrime/`](./task-5-cybercrime/)

**Cybercrime Type: UPI / Online Payment Fraud — The Courier Scam**

Scammers impersonate courier officials and law enforcement officers to create fear, urgency, and authority — pressuring victims into transferring money via UPI to a "government account." Recovery after payment is nearly impossible.

**Stay Safe Online — 12-Point Checklist:**

| # | Tip |
|:---:|:---|
| 1 | Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank |
| 2 | Verify before you transfer — call back on the official number before acting on any "urgent" request |
| 3 | No government agency collects money via UPI — customs, CBI, and courts do not work this way |
| 4 | Hang up on digital arrest calls — no legitimate authority will keep you on a video call for hours |
| 5 | Do not scan unknown QR codes — especially from buyers on OLX or Facebook Marketplace |
| 6 | Use strong, unique passwords for all banking and UPI apps |
| 7 | Check app permissions — no UPI app needs access to your contacts, camera, or microphone |
| 8 | Do not install screen-sharing apps from an unknown caller |
| 9 | Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your bank app to reduce potential loss |
| 10 | Link only one account with a small balance to your UPI app — keep savings in a separate account |
| 11 | Log out of payment apps on shared or borrowed devices immediately after use |
| 12 | Report immediately — every minute counts after a fraudulent transfer |

🚨 **Report Cybercrime:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) &nbsp;|&nbsp; 📞 **Helpline 1930** (24×7, Financial Fraud)
